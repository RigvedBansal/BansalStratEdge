Variance Analysis Template

Monthly Variance Bridge Template

A good bridge does more than reconcile plan to actual. It exposes which movements were real, which were timing, and which now deserve intervention. This template is built to compress the distance between the close and a useful management conversation.

Best For

  • Monthly business review packs
  • CEO or board commentary after a miss or outperformance
  • Teams that want fewer tables and clearer movement logic

Core Outputs

  • Revenue, gross margin, EBITDA, or cash bridge from plan to actual
  • Driver labels that help commentary move beyond "better / worse than budget"
  • Short note on what changed, why, and what follows

Bridge Structure

Keep categories clean enough that management can follow them in one pass.

Bridge item Impact Commentary cue
Opening budget EBITDA INR 2.4 Cr Baseline
Volume miss in enterprise INR -0.7 Cr Deals slipped 3-4 weeks
FX gain on USD billing INR +0.2 Cr Rate moved from 82.8 to 83.5
Cloud cost overrun INR -0.18 Cr Usage spike on data workloads

Commentary Builder

Use the bridge to draft the note, not the other way around.

What changed: EBITDA landed INR 0.68 Cr below plan, mainly from enterprise timing and cloud overrun.

Why: Two large deals shifted into next month while data workloads ran above expected usage.

What follows: Revenue timing remains recoverable, but cloud usage now needs owner review before the next cycle.

Usage Rules

Keep the bridge honest.

  • Use mutually exclusive categories. One movement should not sit in two buckets.
  • Separate timing effects from structural change whenever possible.
  • Bridge only the handful of movements that explain most of the variance.
  • End the bridge with a sentence on action, not just explanation.

Key Logic

Total variance Total variance = Actual result - Budget result
Bridge balance check Opening budget + Sum of bridge movements = Actual result
Timing note If recoverable next cycle, label as timing. If new run-rate changes, label as structural.

Common Mistakes

  • Too many small categories that hide the main story
  • Using the bridge as a data dump instead of a decision tool
  • Mixing revenue, margin, and cash movement without clear order

When This Tool Shines

Use it when management wants the short answer to, "What moved this month and what should we do about it?" without forcing finance to explain ten disconnected tables.

Adapt To Your Context

The bridge only works when the categories mirror how the business actually moves.

Adapt the buckets to your operating model, but do not let the bridge become another report no one can narrate. The main goal is faster judgment. If you want this embedded into your month-end, variance, and board pack rhythm, the toolkit hub is the next step.

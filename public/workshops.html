Flagship Formats

Two workshop formats to start with, both adaptable to your cohort and context.

Sessions can be calibrated for universities, executive education, corporate teams, or curated leadership groups. The material stays the same in spirit but the level, examples, and interaction format can shift with the audience.

2-hour executive workshop

AI in Finance: From Hype to Real Impact

Best for guest lectures, CXO forums, and compact executive cohorts

A concise, high-signal session built to separate genuine finance use cases from AI theater while surfacing the leadership and governance implications that matter most.

  1. 15 min: Setting the context: why AI matters now and how finance is shifting from hindsight to foresight.
  2. 25 min: Where AI is actually working across FP&A, risk, treasury, pricing, and decision support.
  3. 25 min: AI in FP&A as a new decision engine, from variance analysis to predictive insight.
  4. 20 min: The illusion of precision, overfitting, model confidence, and ownership when AI gets it wrong.
  5. 20 min: Implementation playbook covering use-case selection, build versus buy choices, and governance.
  6. 10-15 min: Interactive case discussion, including whether a bank should use AI for capital allocation.
  7. 5 min: Closing perspective on how AI will reshape finance careers and decision expectations.
Participant outcome

Clarity on what is real, what is hype, and how finance leaders can start responsibly.

1-day deep-dive workshop

AI-Driven Finance Transformation: Strategy, Execution, and Leadership

Best for executive education, corporate teams, and immersive cohorts

A full-day applied workshop for participants who want frameworks, implementation logic, and a sharper view of how AI changes finance operating models, capital decisions, and leadership roles.

  1. 60 min: Finance in the age of AI, including the future role of the CFO and the shift toward decision science.
  2. 90 min: AI use cases across FP&A, risk, fraud, treasury, and customer profitability with real-world examples.
  3. 75 min: AI for capital allocation and balance sheet optimization, including RWA, pricing, and ROTCE logic.
  4. 60 min: From data to decision, with focus on where analytics often fail before action is taken.
  5. 60 min: AI risks, governance, regulation, model risk, explainability, and accountability in Basel contexts.
  6. 60 min: Implementation playbook covering use-case identification, data readiness, talent, and change management.
  7. 45-60 min: Interactive case study where participants apply the framework to a banking or finance scenario.
  8. 30 min: Leadership and career implications for modern finance professionals and enterprise teams.
Participant outcome

Actionable frameworks, an implementation roadmap, and a more strategic operating perspective.
